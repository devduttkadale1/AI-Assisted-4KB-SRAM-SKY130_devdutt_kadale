## Peripheral Circuits

Studied major SRAM support circuits:

### Precharge Circuit

Purpose:

* Equalizes bitlines before read operations
* Improves sensing reliability

### Sense Amplifier

Purpose:

* Detects small voltage differences
* Converts analog bitline information into digital outputs

### Row Decoder

Purpose:

* Converts row address bits into one-hot wordline signals
* Selects a single SRAM row

### Column Multiplexer

Purpose:

* Selects one word from multiple words stored within a physical row
* Reduces bitline count and improves array efficiency

# 4. Logical vs Physical Memory Organization

One of the most important concepts learned during Week 1 was the distinction between logical and physical memory organization.

Logical SRAM:

1024 × 32

Physical organization generated by OpenRAM:

128 Rows × 256 Columns

with

8 Words per Row

Key observations:

* Physical organization differs from logical specification.
* Column multiplexing allows multiple words to share bitlines.
* Reduced bitline length improves speed and lowers capacitance.

# 10. Key Takeaways

The most important realization from Week 1 is that OpenRAM does not generate SRAMs from scratch.

OpenRAM combines:

* Memory specifications
* SKY130 technology information
* Pre-designed custom cells

to automatically generate complete SRAM macros and ASIC design views.

A strong understanding of SRAM architecture and memory organization is essential before attempting SRAM generation, characterization, and verification.
